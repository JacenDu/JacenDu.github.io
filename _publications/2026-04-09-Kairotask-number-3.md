Effective personal task management hinges on anchoring abstract intentions to precise spatiotemporal retrieval contexts. However, the ambiguity of user inputs often fractures the link between intent and context, causing tasks to lie dormant due to missed execution windows. To address this, we introduce Kairotask. This mobile probe utilizes Large Language Models to proactively inject spatiotemporal metadata into ambiguous instructions. By introducing benign friction through an inference-confirmation loop, Kairotask assists users in concretizing execution contexts while reinforcing memory encoding. An 8-day field study (𝑁 = 7) suggests that the system functions as effective "cognitive scaffolding," helping users concretize abstract intents into actionable parameters. The study further reveals the nuanced influence of user structuredness: the system effectively compensates for the schedule management cognitive performance of "weak planners," while yielding contrary outcomes for "strong planners." Based on these findings, we discuss design implications for adaptive AI in Personal Information Management.
