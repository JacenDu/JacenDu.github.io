This systematic review focuses on conversational agents (CAs) for older adults in the context of rapid advances in natural language processing and large language model technologies, as well as the growing elderly population. We evaluated and screened 163 articles from seven sources and analyzed 30 of them in depth to answer four key research questions. The review categorizes related research into four key areas: application scenarios (Health & Medical, Daily Life, Competency Improvement, and Psychology & Cognition), functionality and design strategies, user needs, and design evaluation methods. It highlights the multidimensionality and context-dependency of user requirements, emphasizes the importance of Context in design, and suggests future research directions including context-specific investigations, multimodal recognition, continuous learning, social network integration, and interdisciplinary collaboration.
