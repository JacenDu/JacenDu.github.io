Older adults frequently experience sleep difficulties, yet existing Personal Informatics (PI) systems often overlook crucial daytime factors and lack tailored support. This study introduces Circadia, a conversational PI system designed to provide emotional support through dialogue, empowering older adults in self-tracking sleep-related daytime factors. A one-week field trial with eight older adult participants demonstrated that Circadia successfully motivated self-reporting and enhanced self-awareness regarding the links between their daytime activities and sleep. Participants positively received the system, primarily due to the perceived emotional support and companionship from the conversational agent. These findings highlight the critical role of dialogue-based emotional support in PI systems for older adults, offering insights for designing more humanistic and effective health management tools that foster engagement and empowerment.
